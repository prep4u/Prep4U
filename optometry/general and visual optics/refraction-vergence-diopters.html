üî≠ Vergence: Describing the Curvature of Light Rays

Definition:
Vergence is the measure of how much light rays are converging or diverging as they travel.

Mathematical Formula:
V = 1 / d
Where:
V is vergence (in diopters, D)
d is the distance (in meters) from the point where light rays converge or appear to diverge

Types of Vergence:

Type Description Example
Positive Rays converging (toward a point) Convex lens
Zero Rays are parallel Distant light source
Negative Rays diverging (spreading apart) Concave lens

Why It Matters:

  • Eye doctors use vergence to understand how your eye focuses.
  • The eye must accommodate varying vergence to keep images sharp.

üìè Diopters: Measuring Lens Power

Definition:
A diopter (D) is a unit of measurement that expresses the refractive power of a lens. It tells us how strongly a lens can bend (converge or diverge) light.

Diopters (D) = 1 / f
Where f is the focal length in meters

Diopters Are:

  • Positive for convex lenses (converging)
  • Negative for concave lenses (diverging)

Examples:

Lens Type Focal Length Diopters (D) Use Case
Convex lens +0.5 m +2 D Reading glasses
Concave lens -1 m -1 D Myopia correction
Convex lens +0.25 m +4 D Magnifiers

Optometry Example:
If you're prescribed -2.00 D, your eye needs a concave lens to diverge incoming light and refocus it onto the retina.
