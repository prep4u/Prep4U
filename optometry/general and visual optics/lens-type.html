Soft lenses are made from flexible, water-absorbing plastics called hydrogels or silicone hydrogels. They conform easily to the shape of your eye, making them comfortable from the first wear.

  • Comfortable to Wear: Their soft, flexible material allows oxygen to pass through to the cornea.

  • Convenient for Active Lifestyles: Great for sports or physical activities. Less likely to dislodge during sudden movement.
  • Available in Many Modalities: Daily disposables (most hygienic), bi-weekly/monthly options, extended wear (some can be worn overnight).
  • Wide Prescription Range: Suitable for myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism, and presbyopia.

  • Less Durable: Tear more easily than rigid lenses.

  • Higher Risk of Infection: Especially with poor hygiene or extended wear.

  • Their shape corrects minor corneal irregularities.
  • Longer Lifespan: Can last 6 months to a year or more with proper care. Cost-effective in the long run.
  • Better for Eye Health: Smaller size allows more oxygen to reach the eye. Lower risk of infection due to less moisture retention.
