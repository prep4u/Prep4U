1. Scope of Practice

Radiographers must work within their defined scope, meaning:

  • -Performing only procedures they are trained and licensed to do.
  • -Not diagnosing or making clinical decisions beyond their role.
  • -Referring to appropriate professionals when necessary.

Why it matters: Acting outside your scope can lead to patient harm, disciplinary action, and legal consequences.

2. Informed Consent

Before any procedure, patients have the legal right to:

  • -Know what the procedure involves.
  • -Understand the risks and benefits.
  • -Refuse or consent voluntarily.

Radiographers must ensure consent is:

  • -Valid (given by a competent adult or legal guardian)
  • -Informed (after explanation)
  • -Voluntary (not coerced)

Note: In emergencies, implied consent may apply.

3. Radiation Safety Laws

Radiographers are legally bound to minimize radiation exposure through the principles of:

  • -Justification (only perform necessary imaging)
  • -Optimization (use the lowest dose for adequate image quality)
  • -Dose limitation (especially for staff and the public)

This includes:

  • -Proper shielding
  • -Correct use of equipment
  • -Adhering to ALARA (As Low As Reasonably Achievable) principles

4. Confidentiality and Data Protection

Medical images and patient data are protected under privacy laws (like HIPAA in the U.S., GDPR in Europe, or national laws elsewhere). Radiographers must:

  • -Keep patient information private.
  • -Avoid discussing cases in public areas.
  • -Use secure systems for storing and sharing images.

Breach of confidentiality can result in lawsuits, job loss, or criminal charges.

5. Documentation and Record Keeping

Accurate documentation is a legal requirement. Radiographers must:

  • -Record procedure details, exposure factors, and observations.
  • -Note any incidents or patient reactions.
  • -Ensure records are clear, complete, and signed.

Poor documentation can compromise patient care and expose professionals to liability.

  • -Avoid manipulation or pressure.
  • -Empower patients with knowledge and understanding.

2. Beneficence and Non-Maleficence

Beneficence: Always act in the best interest of the patient.

  • -Using safe practices.
  • -Being honest if something goes wrong.

3. Justice and Fairness

Radiographers should treat all patients:

  • -Equally, regardless of age, race, gender, religion, or background.
  • -Without bias or discrimination.
  • -With professionalism and respect, even in difficult situations.

4. Professional Integrity

Ethical radiographers:

  • -Maintain honesty in all professional interactions.
  • -Avoid falsifying records or misrepresenting qualifications.
  • -Report unsafe practices or unethical behavior when witnessed.

Upholding integrity builds public trust and safeguards the profession.
